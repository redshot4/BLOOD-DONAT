Rh Factor Explained

The Rh factor is an inherited protein found on the surface of red blood cells. If your blood has the protein, you're Rh positive (+). If your blood lacks the protein, you're Rh negative (-).

This distinction is vital for pregnancy and transfusions. Generally, Rh-negative patients can only receive Rh-negative blood to avoid an immune reaction.

Why Compatibility Matters

Receiving incompatible blood can cause your immune system to attack the new blood cells, leading to a life-threatening reaction.

Our system uses advanced algorithms to ensure 100% accurate matching between donors and recipients before any transfusion takes place.
