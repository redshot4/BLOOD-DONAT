Privacy Policy

Transparency about how we handle your personal and health data.

Last Updated: January 2024

1. Information We Collect

We collect information you provide directly to us, such as when you create a donor account, schedule a donation, or contact us for support. We prioritize the principle of data minimization, collecting only what is necessary.

This includes:

  • Personal Identifiers: Name, email address, phone number, and date of birth.
  • Health Information: Blood type, relevant medical history, and eligibility status (Protected under HIPAA).
  • Technical Data: Location data for finding nearby donation centers and device information for security purposes.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to operate our platform and save lives. Specifically, we use data to:

  • Facilitate blood donations and match donors with urgent hospital needs.
  • Maintain your secure donor profile and track your life-saving donation history.
  • Send critical alerts about local blood shortages or donation drives.
  • Improve our platform's security, functionality, and user experience.

3. Data Protection & Security

Your trust is paramount. We employ enterprise-grade security measures to protect your data, including:

  • Encryption: All data is encrypted in transit (SSL/TLS) and at rest (AES-256).
  • Compliance: We strictly adhere to HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) and GDPR regulations.
  • Access Control: Only authorized medical and administrative personnel have access to sensitive health data.

4. Third-Party Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We only share data in the following limited circumstances:

  • With healthcare providers (hospitals/clinics) strictly to fulfill your donation process.
  • With service providers who help us operate the platform (under strict confidentiality agreements).
  • When required by law or to protect the safety of our users.

5. Your Rights

You maintain full control over your data. You have the right to:

  • Access your personal data and donation history.
  • Update or correct inaccurate information.
  • Request deletion of your account (subject to medical record retention laws).
  • Opt-out of non-essential communications.

6. Contact Us

If you have specific privacy concerns or wish to exercise your data rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer:

privacy@bloodlife.com
