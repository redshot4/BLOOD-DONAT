Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about eligibility, safety, and the donation process.

General Questions

What is blood donation? ▼

Blood donation is a voluntary procedure where a healthy person donates blood to help patients in need of blood transfusions. The process is quick, safe, and lifesaving.

Why is blood donation important? ▼

Blood is essential for surgeries, emergency care, cancer treatment, and treating patients with blood disorders. One donation can save up to 3 lives.

How often can I donate? ▼

Healthy donors can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet and plasma donations can be made more frequently (up to 24 times a year).

Eligibility & Health

Who can donate blood? ▼

Generally, you must be at least 18 years old (16-17 with parental consent), weigh at least 110 pounds, and be in good health on the day of donation.

Can I donate if I'm taking medication? ▼

Most medications are acceptable. However, some (like blood thinners or acne meds) may require a waiting period. Please check our eligibility tool or ask during screening.

Safety & Side Effects

Is blood donation safe? ▼

Yes. We use sterile, single-use needles for every donor. You cannot contract any disease from donating blood.

Will I feel weak afterwards? ▼

Most people feel fine. You may feel a little lightheaded if you haven't eaten or hydrated enough. We provide snacks and juice to help you recover quickly.
